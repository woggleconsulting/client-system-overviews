Section 1

Overview

This is the single reference for the automated system behind your client journey in Zoho. The journey runs in six phases, from a first inquiry through drafting, signing, and the three-year review. One automated path carries each matter along — with you at every decision point, and your own SOPs as the blueprint underneath.

Each phase below is documented the same way: a short description of what happens, a table of the components in the system and their status, and — where something needs your input — a Review prompt right on the item, plus an Action required box for anything to provide or decide. Behind these client-facing emails, the same automations also create the staff and attorney tasks that move each matter along — those are catalogued in Internal tasks & reminders. Nothing here is urgent; work through it at your own pace.

One distinction worth keeping in mind as you read: “the automation is built” and “the wording is final” are two different things. An automation can be finished and tested while the copy inside its emails still needs your sign-off — so a built item can still be waiting on a word or two from you. Where that's the case, it's flagged.

⚑ Updated June 8This version reflects our June 8 walkthrough. A few automations built early on the old “Leads” setup are being cleaned up and re-pointed to the matter — you’ll see those flagged in-line. I’ll keep this page updated as we go.
Section 2

How to use this document

Read top to bottom, or jump to a phase from the contents above. Anything that needs your input is one of three kinds:

▸ ReviewSits right under the component it concerns. Open the finished item, read it the way a client would, and confirm it's right. Each one links straight into the template in Zoho.
✎ ProvideFlagged right on any message that's still empty (and in the Action required box). The message links to its template in Zoho; you add the wording in your content doc.
DecideAlso in Action required — make a small choice. Each has a recommended default you can simply approve.

Underlined text is a link. Checkboxes are there so you can tick items off as you go.
